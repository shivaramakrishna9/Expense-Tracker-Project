Expense Tracker Project Description
Overview
Expense Tracker is a personal finance management web application designed to help individuals take control of their day-to-day spending, monitor their income, and work toward achieving their monthly savings goals. The application provides a structured, user-friendly environment where anyone can record their earnings and expenditures, visualize where their money is going, and understand how close they are to reaching a savings target they set for themselves.
Purpose and Problem Statement
Many people struggle to understand where their money goes each month. Salaries come in, bills go out, and by the end of the month, very little is left often without a clear explanation. The Expense Tracker solves this by giving users a single, organized place to record every rupee earned and spent, so they always know their financial position in real time.
Who Is This For?
This application is built for everyday individuals, students, salaried professionals, freelancers, or homemakers who want a simple and private tool to manage personal finances without the complexity of spreadsheets or accounting software.


How the Application Works 
1. Registration
A new user begins by creating an account. During registration, they provide:
Full name, email address, and a secure password — for account identity and login.
Basic personal details — such as age, gender, and occupation (optional, for profile context).
Monthly income — the total amount the user earns in a month (salary, freelance income, allowance, etc.).
Recurring fixed expenses — bills that are paid every month without variation, such as rent, EMIs, electricity, internet, subscriptions, or insurance premiums. These are entered once and automatically accounted for every month.
Monthly savings goal — the exact amount in rupees that the user wants to have saved by the end of the month. This is the most important figure the app works toward.
2. Login
Returning users log in with their email and password. The application securely authenticates them and takes them directly to their personal dashboard.
3. The Dashboard
The dashboard is the central screen of the application. It gives the user an at-a-glance financial summary for the current month, including:
Total income for the month.
Total recurring expenses (pre-filled from registration).
Total variable expenses entered so far.
Current savings (income minus all expenses).
A savings goal progress indicator showing how close the user is to their target — displayed as a progress bar or percentage, e.g., "You have saved ₹4,200 of your ₹8,000 goal — 52% achieved."
4. Logging Expenses
Throughout the month, the user manually records every expense they make. Each expense entry requires:
Category — the type of spending. Pre-defined categories include:
 Food & Dining
 Shopping
 Transport
 Health & Medical
 Entertainment
 Education
 Utilities & Bills
Travel
 Gifts & Donations
 Miscellaneous
Amount — how much was spent.
Date — when the expense occurred.
Note (optional) — a short description, e.g., "Lunch at office canteen" or "New running shoes."
5. Savings Calculation
The application continuously calculates the user's savings using the formula:
Savings = Monthly Income − Recurring Fixed Expenses − Sum of All Variable Expenses Logged
This figure is compared against the user's savings goal to show how much more needs to be saved or whether the goal has already been exceeded.
6. Profile Management
The user can revisit their profile at any time to:
Update personal details.
Adjust monthly income (e.g., if they received a bonus or changed jobs).
Add, edit, or remove recurring expenses.
Revise their monthly savings goal.
